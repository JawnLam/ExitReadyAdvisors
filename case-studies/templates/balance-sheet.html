Notes to Financial Statements:

Note 1 - Basis of Presentation: These financial statements are prepared on the accrual basis of accounting in accordance with U.S. GAAP. The percentage-of-completion method is used for long-term construction contracts.

Note 2 - Working Capital: Net Working Capital as of December 31, 2024: $4,500,000 (Current Assets of $17,500,000 less Current Liabilities of $13,000,000)

Note 3 - Project Backlog: The company has approximately $88,000,000 in signed contracts and awarded projects not yet reflected in revenue, providing significant revenue visibility for the next 18-24 months.

Note 4 - Credit Facilities: The company maintains a $5,000,000 revolving credit facility secured by accounts receivable and inventory. The facility bears interest at Prime + 1.5% and matures in 2026.

Note 5 - Interest-Bearing Debt: Total interest-bearing debt as of December 31, 2024: $9,750,000, consisting of the revolving line of credit ($5,000,000), equipment loans ($2,250,000), and mortgage notes ($2,500,000).

Note 6 - Revenue Recognition: The company recognizes revenue using the percentage-of-completion method, measured by the percentage of costs incurred to date to estimated total costs for each contract.

Note 7 - Property & Equipment: Depreciation is computed using the straight-line method over the following estimated useful lives: Buildings (30 years), Equipment (5-10 years), Vehicles (5 years), Office Equipment (3-7 years). The three owned facilities (Phoenix, Salt Lake City, and Denver) totaling approximately 75,000 sq. ft. are included in the sale.

Note 8 - Income Taxes: As an S-Corporation, the company's income is passed through to its shareholders for federal income tax purposes. The company is subject to certain state and local taxes.

Note 9 - Subsequent Events: The company has evaluated subsequent events through the date these financial statements were available to be issued and determined no material events requiring disclosure have occurred.
