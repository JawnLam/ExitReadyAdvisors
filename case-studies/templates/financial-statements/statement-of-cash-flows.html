Note 1: Basis of Presentation
The Statement of Cash Flows has been prepared using the indirect method in accordance with ASC 230. This method reconciles net income to net cash provided by operating activities by adjusting for non-cash items and changes in operating assets and liabilities. As a specialty contractor using percentage-of-completion accounting, significant working capital fluctuations are normal based on project timing and milestone billings.
Note 2: Construction Industry Working Capital Dynamics
The company's operating cash flow reflects typical construction industry patterns. The increase in accounts receivable of $750,000 and contract assets of $480,000 represents growth in the business and timing of project milestones. The corresponding increases in accounts payable ($425,000) and contract liabilities ($440,000) demonstrate balanced working capital management. The net working capital use of $473,000 is reasonable given 5.6% revenue growth.
Note 3: Capital Expenditure Strategy
Capital expenditures of $550,000 (0.8% of revenue) are below the construction industry average of 1.5-2.0%, reflecting the company's mature asset base and efficient equipment utilization. Fleet vehicle additions of $350,000 represent planned replacements to maintain the 115-vehicle fleet. The lower capex suggests potential deferred investments that a new owner may need to address, particularly in technology systems noted in the business analysis.
Note 4: S-Corporation Distribution Policy
Total distributions of $4,635,000 comprise regular quarterly distributions of $4,000,000 (77.7% of net income) and tax distributions of $635,000 to cover shareholders' pass-through tax obligations. The distribution coverage ratio of 1.99x (operating cash flow to total distributions) demonstrates strong cash generation capacity while maintaining adequate working capital for bonding requirements.
Note 5: Debt Management
The net increase in line of credit borrowings of $500,000 supports working capital needs for the growing project backlog. Equipment loan proceeds of $425,000 financed fleet additions, while maintaining a conservative debt structure. Total debt service of $1,325,000 (principal and interest) represents only 14.4% of operating cash flow, indicating strong debt service coverage of 6.95x.
Note 6: Multi-State Cash Management
Cash is managed across three operating locations with separate banking relationships: Wells Fargo (Phoenix - 50%), Zions Bank (Salt Lake City - 30%), and FirstBank (Denver - 20%). This structure optimizes cash management for multi-state operations while maintaining local banking relationships critical for project financing and bonding support.
Note 7: Significant Non-Cash Adjustments
Total depreciation and amortization of $1,218,000 includes $850,000 for tangible assets and $368,000 for intangible assets (customer relationships, trade name, and non-compete agreements). These non-cash charges reflect the company's significant fixed asset base and acquisition-related intangibles from historical transactions.
