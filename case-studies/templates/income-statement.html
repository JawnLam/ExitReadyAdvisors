Notes to Income Statement:

Note 1 - Revenue Recognition: Revenue is recognized using the percentage-of-completion method for construction contracts. The company uses the cost-to-cost method as its primary measure of progress. Service revenue is recognized as services are performed. Provisions for estimated losses on uncompleted contracts are made in the period in which such losses are determined.
Note 2 - Cost Classifications: Direct project costs including materials, subcontractors, direct labor, equipment rental, job bonds, and project-specific insurance are included in Cost of Revenues. Project management salaries, estimating costs, and general overhead are included in SG&A expenses.
Note 3 - Adjusted EBITDA: Represents earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization, adjusted for owner compensation in excess of market rates and one-time transaction-related expenses. The owner compensation adjustment of $4,950,000 (2024) and $4,850,000 (2023) reflects the difference between current owner draws/compensation and estimated market-rate replacement management costs of approximately $450,000-$500,000 annually.
Note 4 - Gross Margin Compression: The decline in gross margin from 19.4% to 17.0% reflects increased competition on larger bid projects, rising material costs (particularly steel and copper), and strategic pricing decisions to maintain market share. Note that the actual TTM gross margin of 20.0% referenced in other documents includes adjustments for certain owner perquisites classified in COGS.
Note 5 - Union vs. Non-Union Labor: Approximately 65% of field labor costs are union-related. The company maintains strong relationships with UA Local 469 (Plumbers & Pipefitters) and Sheet Metal Workers Local 359.
Note 6 - Subcontractor Concentration: No single subcontractor represents more than 8% of total subcontractor costs. The company maintains preferred vendor agreements with 15+ specialty trade partners.
Note 7 - Warranty Reserve: The company maintains a warranty reserve based on historical callback rates of approximately 0.2% of revenue. The improvement in 2024 reflects enhanced quality control procedures implemented in late 2023.
Note 8 - Customer Concentration: The top customer represented 14% of 2024 revenue. The top 10 customers collectively represented approximately 70% of revenue, with strong retention rates exceeding 95%.
Note 9 - Backlog Composition: Of the $88 million backlog, approximately 60% is expected to be completed within 12 months. The backlog includes $52 million in multifamily residential, $21 million in hospitality, $10 million in industrial, and $5 million in other commercial projects.
Note 10 - Change Order Management: The decrease in change order revenue reflects improved project scoping and the company's shift toward negotiated contracts with more comprehensive initial specifications.
