Business Description

Johnson & Sons Heating, Plumbing, & Air is a premier mechanical contractor with a distinguished 45-year history of excellence in the HVAC and plumbing sectors. The company specializes in serving commercial, industrial, and institutional clients across a three-state operational footprint in the southwestern United States. With a robust portfolio of over 3,500 completed construction units annually and a backlog of $88 million in contracted projects, it has established itself as a trusted partner for some of the largest national owner/builders. The firm's success is built on a foundation of near-perfect client retention, a highly scalable operational model, and a seasoned leadership team.

History & Context

Founded by William Johnson in 1979, the company began as a small family operation with a single truck. William's commitment to quality and customer service laid the groundwork for what would become a regional leader in mechanical contracting. The business is now in its second generation, with the same founding principles guiding its expansion and success.

Market & Competitive Landscape

  • Market Position: A dominant regional player with a service area covering high-growth markets in the Southwest, positioned to capitalize on continued commercial and industrial development.
  • Competitive Advantage: Unlike smaller, local competitors, the company's multi-state footprint, sophisticated project management systems, and four-decade-long relationships with national builders create a significant competitive moat.
  • Growth Opportunities: The company has clear, actionable pathways for expansion, including entering the robust Texas market, increasing its footprint in the hospitality sector, and launching a tech-forward division focused on smart building systems and energy-efficient solutions.

Customer Portfolio Deep Dive

  • Client Retention: The company boasts a near-100% repeat business rate from its core clients, reflecting deep trust and consistent project execution.
  • Contract Structure: Long-term master service agreements are in place with key clients, providing a predictable and recurring revenue stream.
  • Project Backlog: With $88 million in active and awarded work, the company has strong revenue visibility for the next 18-24 months.
  • Concentration Risk: The client base is well-diversified, with no single client accounting for more than 15% of annual revenue, mitigating concentration risk.
  • Case Studies: A portfolio of completed large-scale projects is available, demonstrating the company's ability to manage complex, multi-million-dollar installations profitably and on schedule.

Operational Assets & Facilities

  • Facilities: The company operates from three strategically located facilities in Phoenix, Salt Lake City, and Denver, all of which are owned and included in the sale.
  • Equipment (FF&E): The sale includes a well-maintained fleet of vehicles and state-of-the-art equipment valued at approximately $5.4 million, with minimal near-term capital expenditure requirements.
  • Inventory Strategy: A robust inventory management system ensures that essential components and materials are readily available, minimizing project delays and enhancing service efficiency.

Technology & Operational Systems

  • Project Management: The company utilizes advanced project management software to streamline scheduling, resource allocation, and budget tracking, ensuring operational efficiency across all projects.
  • Proprietary Processes: Over the past 45 years, Johnson & Sons has developed proprietary installation techniques and quality control checklists that ensure consistent, high-quality outcomes, differentiating the company from its competitors.
  • Scalability: The current operational infrastructure and systems are highly scalable, supporting significant growth without requiring major additional investment.

Management & Workforce

  • Leadership Team: A seasoned and fully autonomous leadership team manages daily operations, with an average tenure of over 15 years. This team is capable of driving growth and executing the company's strategic vision independently.
  • Workforce: The company employs a dedicated team of over 110 skilled technicians, project managers, and support staff. Low employee turnover and a strong company culture are central to its success.
  • Training & Development: Johnson & Sons invests heavily in continuous training and professional development, ensuring its team remains at the forefront of industry standards and new technologies.
  • Owner Dependence: The business is not dependent on the owner for day-to-day operations, with established systems and a strong management team in place to ensure a seamless transition of leadership.

Transaction

  • Structure: The transaction is anticipated as an asset sale to provide a step-up in basis for the buyer; however, a stock sale may also be considered.
  • Financing: A combination of third-party debt and seller financing is proposed to facilitate the acquisition.
  • Seller Role: The owner will commit to a 24-36 month transition period in a full-time capacity, followed by a flexible advisory role.
  • Working Capital: A normalized net working capital target will be established and adjusted at closing.
  • Escrow: A portion of the proceeds will be held in escrow to cover any potential post-closing indemnification claims.

Transition

  • Continuity: The existing leadership team, with an average tenure of over 15 years, will remain to ensure operational stability and continuity.
  • Knowledge Transfer: A comprehensive transition plan will be executed to transfer all critical knowledge, systems access, and client relationships.
  • Employee Retention: Key employee retention agreements will be established to secure top talent following the acquisition.
  • Client Communication: A joint communication strategy will be developed to inform clients and stakeholders of the ownership change, ensuring the integrity of the relationship.

Key Transaction Issues

  • Seller Role: The owner's 24-36 month transition commitment is critical for transferring key relationships and strategic knowledge.
  • Contract Novation: Transferring the $88M backlog and master service agreements will require proactive client consent and negotiation.
  • Management Retention: Securing the long-tenured leadership team post-acquisition is essential for operational stability and continued growth.
  • Real Estate: The sale of three owned operational hubs requires separate valuation and presents an opportunity for a sale-leaseback arrangement.
  • Working Capital: The $4.5M net working capital peg is a key financial term that will require careful due diligence and negotiation.

Valuation

The asking price of $51,250,000 is based on a multiple of Adjusted EBITDA, reflecting the company's strong market position, recurring revenue, and significant growth potential. A detailed valuation report is available to qualified buyers.
